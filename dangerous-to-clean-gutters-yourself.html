How dangerous is it to clean your own gutters?

More dangerous than most people think. Falls from ladders send well over 160,000 Americans to the emergency room every year and kill roughly 300, according to U.S. safety data — and gutter cleaning is one of the most common reasons homeowners are up on a ladder in the first place.

The risk climbs fast on two-story homes and on the uneven, sandy ground a lot of Florida houses sit on. And it isn't only the high falls that hurt people — most ladder injuries happen from 10 feet or less, and a short fall onto concrete or a paver patio still means fractures, head injuries, or worse. It only takes one slip, one soft spot in the soil, or one overreach.

Here's the reframe: the safest gutter is the one you never have to climb up to. With gutter guards and professional service, you never get on a ladder for your gutters again. That's the real value — not just clean gutters, but your safety and never having to risk a fall to keep your home protected.
